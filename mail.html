Mailing Lists

Thanks to Martin Maechler, there are three mailing lists devoted to R:

r-announce
This list is for announcements about the development of R and the availability of new code.
The list has a low volume (about one a week) and everyone mildly interested should consider subscribing, but note that R-help gets everything from R-announce as well, so you don't need to subscribe to both of them. As a very mild form of moderation, you can only post to R-announce when you are subscribed to either R-announce or R-help.
Note that we consider making R-announce completely "read-only" for the public, to be used for announcements mainly by the R Core Development Team.

r-help
The `main' R mailing list, for announcements about the development of R and the availability of new code, questions and answers about problems and solutions using R, enhancements and patches to the source code and documentation of R, comparison and compatibility with S and S-plus, and for the posting of nice examples and benchmarks.
This has become quite an active list with often dozens of messages per day. An alternative is to subscribe to r-help-digest instead, see below.

Archives: 1997 1998 1999 2000 2001 2002
 
r-devel
This list is for discussions about the future of R and pre-testing of new versions. It is meant for those who maintain an active position in the development of R.

General Instructions

To send a message to everyone on the r-help mailing list, send email to r-help@lists.R-project.org.
Note that you should configure your e-mail software in such a way as to send only plain text, i.e. no HTML. ``HTML''ified messages are usually considerably longer (in bytes!) and harder to filter for spam. Many of these are currently filtered and would need manual interaction of the list maintainer.

To subscribe (or unsubscribe) to this list send ``subscribe'' (or ``unsubscribe'') in the body of the message (not in the subject!) to r-help-request@lists.R-project.org . Information about the list can be obtained by sending an email with ``info'' as its contents to r-help-request@lists.R-project.org .

Subscription and posting to the other lists is done analogously, with `r-help' replaced by `r-announce' and `r-devel', respectively. Note that the r-announce list is gatewayed into r-help, so you don't need to subscribe to both of them.

It is recommended that you send mail to r-help rather than only to the R developers (who are also subscribed to the list, of course). This may save them precious time they can use for constantly improving R, and will typically also result in much quicker feedback for yourself.

Of course, in the case of bug reports it would be very helpful to have code which reliably reproduces the problem, see the entry in the R FAQ.

List Digests

Subscriptions to `r-help' and `r-devel' are also available in digest format. With this option, several messages are compiled into a single currently daily message for convenient reading by those who do not prefer immediate receipt of individual messages.
NOTE (2002-11-15): The R mailing lists are being transfered from the majordomo to the mailman software. There, digestification is a switch you can turn on or off.
2002-11-18: R-devel is now managed via mailman. The R-devel-digest list no longer exists separately.
To get messages in digest format for R-help, currently subscribe to the separate digest list `r-help-digest'.
Since this will change very soon, you may consider waiting for that.
To subscribe to r-help in digest format, send an email with the word ``subscribe'' in the body of the message (not in the subject!) to r-help-digest-request@lists.R-project.org.
